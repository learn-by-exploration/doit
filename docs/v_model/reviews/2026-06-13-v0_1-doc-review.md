# v0.1 Documentation Review

**Date:** 2026-06-13.
**Reviewer:** Claude (self-review of left-side V-Model artifacts).
**Scope:** All 12 V-Model docs, 3 top-level files, 7 path-scoped rules.
**Method:** Manual re-read, cross-reference of SYS-IDs / WF-IDs / ADR
IDs, consistency check across the V, and gap analysis against the
v0.1 baseline acceptance criteria.

## Summary

The left side of the V is **largely consistent and complete**, but
there are 7 inconsistencies, 10 spec/requirement gaps, 3 missing
ADRs, and 5 minor doc-quality cleanups. None of them invalidate the
baseline; they all refine it. Total: **25 findings** (3 critical,
12 moderate, 10 minor).

A separate note: the **14-day acceptance test set in
`v0_1_baseline.md`** exercises only 3 of the 5 mission types
(Shake, Hold-tap, Type). Math and Memory ship in the engine but
are not part of the acceptance run. This is probably the right
call (the acceptance set is for verifying the loop, not exhaustively
exercising every mission) but should be stated explicitly.

## A. Cross-doc inconsistencies (7) — apply all

### A1. `WF-001 step 9` says "three default presets"; should be 4

**File:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:36`
**Issue:** Step 9 says "shows the home screen with three default
presets ready to accept (drink water, call Mom, morning routine)".
The v0.1 baseline defines **4** presets (drink water, call
person, morning routine, daily todo). Daily Todo is missing from
the onboarding list.

**Proposed change:** Add "Daily Todo" to the list. (WF-001 also
references "call Mom" specifically; should be "call &lt;person&gt;"
or "call person" to match the preset naming.)

### A2. `WF-008` vs `WF-014` — inconsistent heads-up copy for the same event

**Files:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:288-294` and
`docs/v_model/workflows.md:456-477`

**Issue:** Both workflows document the first-unlock wake-up. The
heads-up copy differs:
- WF-008: "Did you just wake up? Tap to confirm."
- WF-014: "Wake-up recorded at 07:12. Anchor morning routine to this?"

WF-014's copy is better (gives the timestamp; names the habit).
WF-008 should be aligned with WF-014, and one of them should be the
canonical copy.

**Proposed change:** Pick WF-014's copy as canonical; update WF-008
to match. Note in WF-014 that it is the canonical wording.

### A3. Brand voice defined in `conops.md` but workflows don't consistently apply it

**Files:** `docs/v_model/conops.md:13-37` (brand voice section)
vs `docs/v_model/workflows.md` (notification copy in WF-004..WF-013)

**Issue:** The brand voice says:
- "Lead with the action" (drink water, not "do it: hydrate")
- "do it number is the period, not the point"
- "No shame" (missed, not broke)

But workflow examples like "do it window open — drink water"
(WF-007 step 1) and "Done — strong proof" (WF-006 step 4) use
"do it" as a prefix or suffix, which is what the brand voice says
to avoid.

**Proposed change:** Update workflow notification copy to lead with
the action; add a one-line note in each workflow that the
notification copy must follow the brand voice in `conops.md`.

### A4. `WF-001 step 7` backup location is correct, but the daily todo preset is missing

**File:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:32`

**Issue:** Same as A1 (related). Step 9 explicitly lists only 3
presets. Should list 4.

**Proposed change:** Covered by A1.

### A5. `WF-006` says "3 wrong in a row = nudge" — not pinned anywhere

**File:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:236`

**Issue:** This is a UX policy that affects implementation. It's

**Proposed change:** See B2 (add a verification clause to SYS-011

### A6. `WF-012` backup retry "3 times with backoff" — not pinned anywhere

**Issue:** This is an implementation behavior. Mentioned only in

**Proposed change:** See B4.

### A7. `WF-009` snooze cap "3 times per occurrence" — not pinned anywhere

**Issue:** UX policy. Not in SYS-018 (snooze).

## B. Spec / requirement gaps (10) — moderate to critical

### B1. Mission chain total timeout cap (5 min) — NEW SYS

**Where defined:** `docs/v_model/mission_catalog.md:317`
("Validation (at habit-save time)" section).

**Issue:** The 5-minute cap is a model validation rule, not a
system requirement. A reader of `requirements.md` has no way to
know this is a hard limit.

**Proposed change:** Add **SYS-031**:

> **SYS-031** The mission chain total timeout (sum of per-mission
> `timeout` values) shall not exceed 5 minutes at habit-save time.
> Longer chains shall require explicit user confirmation in v0.2.
> Verification: `test/missions/chain_test.dart` validates that a
> chain with timeout sum > 5 min throws on save.

Update `traceability_matrix.md` workflow table (WF-002 covers
habit-save validation).

### B2. Math mission "3-wrong nudge" — expand SYS-011

**Where mentioned:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:236` (WF-006
failure mode).

**Issue:** Specific UX behavior, not in SYS-011.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-011** to add: "The Math mission
shall log every wrong answer; after 3 consecutive wrong answers on
the same problem, the app shall surface a 'take a break' nudge and
auto-fail the mission (no fourth attempt)."

### B3. Snooze max 3 per occurrence — expand SYS-018

**Where mentioned:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:325-327`
(WF-009 step 5).

**Issue:** Specific UX behavior, not in SYS-018.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-018** to add: "A snoozed
reminder may be snoozed up to 3 times within a single occurrence;
further snooze taps shall surface 'you've snoozed 3 times — skip
or do it' and offer no further snooze option."

### B4. Backup retry "3 times with backoff" — expand SYS-023

**Where mentioned:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:418-419`
(WF-012 failure mode).

**Issue:** Implementation behavior, not in SYS-023.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-023** to add: "If the SAF write
fails, the app shall retry up to 3 times with exponential backoff
(1 s, 5 s, 30 s). If all retries fail, a banner shall be shown
and the user notified via a 'last backup failed' status in
Settings."

### B5. Notification channel "streak.reminders" — NEW SYS or expand SYS-005

**Where defined:** `docs/v_model/architecture_options.md` (mentioned
informally).

**Issue:** The channel is mentioned in architecture but not in
any SYS or workflow.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-005** to add: "The app shall
create one or more Android notification channels for reminders
(default channel id: `streak.reminders`, importance HIGH). The
user may configure sound and vibration per-channel in the system
settings."

### B6. Duplicate habit name forbidden — expand SYS-001

**Where defined:** `docs/v_model/open_questions.md:9`
(current answer: "Forbid duplicates. The model throws
`DuplicateHabitName`; the UI shows a suggestion.").

**Issue:** Defined in open questions but not pinned in a SYS or
ADR.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-001** to add: "Habit names
shall be unique within the local DB (case-insensitive, trim). A
duplicate shall be rejected at save time with
`DuplicateHabitName`." Cross-link to A/C: close OQ-9 and add a
note that the policy is pinned by SYS-001.

### B7. "I'm up" debounce (4 hours) — expand SYS-015

**Where mentioned:** `docs/v_model/conops.md:42`, `v0_1_baseline.md:115`,
`workflows.md:299-300`.

**Issue:** Debounce is mentioned in three places but not in SYS-015.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-015** to add: "A 4-hour debounce
shall prevent a second wake-up anchor event from being recorded
within 4 hours of the previous one."

### B8. Grace window default (03:00) — expand SYS-019

**Where mentioned:** `docs/v_model/v0_1_baseline.md:86-87`.

**Issue:** Default grace window is "until 03:00 next day" but
SYS-019 says only "A streak breaks only on a missed day past
the grace window" without pinning the default.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-019** to add: "The default
grace window shall be 03:00 local time the next day; a habit
completed between 00:00 and 03:00 shall count for the previous
day. The grace window is per-habit configurable."

### B9. Backup file format version "1" — expand SYS-023

**Where mentioned:** `docs/v_model/v0_1_baseline.md:137`.

**Issue:** Format version is "1" but SYS-023 just says "versioned
JSON file".

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-023** to add: "The backup file
format shall be `{"version": 1, "exported_at": <ISO8601>,
"data": {...}}`. Files with a higher `version` shall be rejected
with 'backup from a newer version' error. Files with a lower
`version` shall be migrated forward via `lib/services/db/migrations/`."

### B10. Shake-N default parameters — expand SYS-008

**Where defined:** `docs/v_model/mission_catalog.md:53-61`.

**Issue:** Defaults (14.0 m/s², 250 ms, 1500 ms) are documented in
the catalog but not pinned in SYS-008 or `v0_1_baseline.md`.

**Proposed change:** Update **SYS-008** to add: "The Shake-N
mission shall use a default magnitude threshold of 14.0 m/s²,
minimum inter-shake spacing of 250 ms, and maximum inter-shake
spacing of 1500 ms. These defaults may be overridden per-habit;
all three values are immutable after the habit has at least one
completion (see ADR-012)."

## C. Decision-record gaps (3) — add 3 ADRs

### C1. ADR-013 — Brand voice

**Issue:** The brand voice was added to `conops.md` as a
subsection but has no ADR. Decisions that affect notification copy
should be recorded.

**Proposed change:** Add **ADR-013** to `decision_record.md`:
"Brand voice: lead with the action, streak number is the period,
no shame. Notification copy is the surface; the docs in
`conops.md § Brand voice` are the source of truth. SYS-IDs
affected: SYS-005, SYS-021."

### C2. ADR-014 — Onboarding permission order

**Issue:** WF-001 step 1-8 has a specific order of permission
requests (notifications → contacts → exact-alarm → battery →
OEM → backup folder → anchor preference). This is not just a
workflow detail — it's a UX policy. The rationale (notifications
first because they're the most important for the app to work;
contacts second because Call Person is a headline preset; exact
alarm third because it's the reliability key; battery and OEM
later because they're OEM-specific; backup later because it's
optional) should be an ADR.

**Proposed change:** Add **ADR-014** to `decision_record.md`:
"Onboarding permission order. Notifications first, contacts
second, exact-alarm third, battery optimization fourth, OEM
auto-start fifth, backup folder sixth, wake-up anchor seventh.
Rationale: each step is contingent on the previous one being
understood by the user. SYS-IDs affected: SYS-025, WF-001."

### C3. ADR-015 — Retry and nudge policies

**Issue:** Multiple specific retry/nudge policies are scattered
across workflows:
- Math: 3 wrong = nudge + auto-fail (WF-006)
- Snooze: 3 max per occurrence (WF-009)
- Backup: 3 retries with backoff (WF-012)

These are user-facing UX policies. They should be in one ADR for
visibility, since they share a "3 strikes" pattern.

**Proposed change:** Add **ADR-015** to `decision_record.md`:
"Three-strikes policy. Where the user is given a finite number of
attempts (wrong math answers, snoozes, backup writes), the default
is 3 attempts. Beyond that, the app nudges or fails over to a
manual fallback. The 3-strike count is a UX default; specific
policies are pinned in SYS-011 (math), SYS-018 (snooze), SYS-023
(backup)."

## E. Doc-quality cleanups (5) — minor

### E1. `SYS-019` and `SYS-021` verifications don't name a test function

**File:** `docs/v_model/requirements.md:97, 99`

**Issue:** The traceability matrix and `AGENTS.md` reference
specific test files for SYS-019 and SYS-021, but the requirement

**Proposed change:** Update SYS-019 verification to: "Unit test

### E2. `WF-011` lists stats not pinned in SYS-021

**File:** `docs/v_model/workflows.md:380-384`

**Issue:** WF-011 lists "Total habits hit today" and "All-time
strongest habit" but SYS-021 does not name them.

**Proposed change:** Either remove them from WF-011 (to match
SYS-021) or expand SYS-021 to include them. Recommend expanding
SYS-021 to keep the stats screen's value.

### E3. Kotlin receiver testing strategy unclear

**File:** `.claude/rules/lib-reminders.md:108-112`

**Issue:** The boot receiver is a Kotlin `BroadcastReceiver`.
Flutter integration tests can verify the Dart side; the Kotlin
side is best tested with a JVM unit test (Robolectric or a direct
`Context` mock). This is not pinned in the rules or in a SYS.

**Proposed change:** Add a paragraph to `lib-reminders.md` (or
SYS-016 verification) that says: "The Kotlin `BootReceiver` is
tested with a Robolectric `BroadcastReceiver` unit test in
`android/app/src/test/kotlin/.../BootReceiverTest.kt`; the Dart
side is tested with `tester.runAsync` in
`test/reminders/reboot_survival_test.dart`."

### E4. 14-day acceptance set uses only 3 of 5 mission types

**File:** `docs/v_model/v0_1_baseline.md:148-162`

**Issue:** The acceptance set exercises Shake (Call Mom), Hold
(Morning routine), and Type (Read 20 min). Math and Memory are
shipped in the engine but not in the acceptance set.

**Proposed change:** Add a one-line note: "Math and Memory are
shipped in the engine and exercised in unit tests
(`test/missions/math_test.dart`, `test/missions/memory_test.dart`),
but the 14-day real-device run is not required to exercise them.
The unit tests are the acceptance for those two types in v0.1."

### E5. Schedule engine contract not explicit on when `nextOccurrence` is called

**File:** `docs/v_model/lib-habits.md` and `notification_reliability.md`

**Issue:** `nextOccurrence()` is documented in `.claude/rules/lib-habits.md`
as pure, but the question of *when* it is called (app launch,
timezone change, anchor event, habit-edit) is not pinned.

**Proposed change:** Add to `notification_reliability.md` a small
"Schedule re-computation triggers" section that enumerates the 4
events that call `nextOccurrence`:
1. App launch (or foreground, after a long background).
2. `ACTION_TIMEZONE_CHANGED`.
3. Anchor event recorded (only for `HabitAnchor`).
4. Habit edited (the schedule is re-armed from the new config).

## F. Implementation notes (not blocking)

These are observations, not findings; they will surface during
implementation.

### F1. Chained anchors

**Where implied:** `WF-002 step 9` says "anchor must reference an
existing anchor habit" — does that allow `Habit A` anchored to
`Habit B` which is anchored to wake-up? v0.1 should probably
disallow chained anchors (an anchor's target must be a wake-up
event, not another habit).

**Action:** Decide in Phase 1 (models) implementation. Update
SYS-004 if the answer differs from current.

### F2. Drift reactive streams and the home screen

**Where implied:** `ADR-011` mentions `ChangeNotifier` /
`ValueNotifier` integration. The home screen is a `StatefulWidget`
per `.claude/rules/lib-screens.md`. The integration pattern
(Drift `Stream` → `ChangeNotifier` → widget) is not pinned.

**Action:** Decide in Phase 2 (DB) implementation. Document the
pattern in `.claude/rules/lib-services.md` once decided.

### F3. Notification "Dismiss" vs "Done" action for strong habits

**Where implied:** `WF-004 step 2` says "Done" / "Open" action.
For strong habits, the "Done" action should not be available
(since proof is required). The action is "Open", which launches
the full-screen intent.

**Action:** Pin in WF-004 step 2 wording during Phase 4
implementation. May not need a SYS change.

### F4. Battery optimization prompt timing

**Where implied:** `WF-001 step 5` prompts on first onboarding.
But the user may not have any fixed-time habits yet at that point
(they only just enabled presets). The prompt is most relevant
after the first fixed-time habit is added. Should the prompt
move to "on first schedule of a fixed-time habit"?

**Action:** Decide in Phase 4 (reminders) implementation. Update
WF-001 if the answer differs. `v0_1_baseline.md § Reliability`
already says "on first scheduling of a fixed-time habit", so
WF-001 is inconsistent with the baseline.
